## Why these competitors, not others

ScopeWise's actual wedge — AI review of a SOW/RFP **before signature** — sits
between three categories that each solve an adjacent problem. None of them
is a 1:1 competitor; each competes for a slice of the same searcher's
attention at a different point in their workflow.

## Category 1: CLM (Contract Lifecycle Management)

| Domain | What they own | Est. relative authority | SEO angle they use |
|---|---|---|---|
| ironclad.com | Enterprise CLM, AI redlining, workflow | Very high (Series E, market leader) | Heavy content marketing: "state of contracting" reports, ROI calculators, glossary, extensive `/resources` |
| icertis.com | Enterprise CLM, large-account focus | Very high | Analyst-report-driven content (Gartner/Forrester citations), enterprise case studies |
| linksquares.com | Mid-market CLM + AI contract analytics | High | Comparison pages (`vs Ironclad`, `vs Icertis`), templates library |
| evisort.com | AI contract analysis/CLM | High | AI-angle content, "AI contract review" keyword cluster |
| contractpodai.com | Enterprise CLM | High | Similar analyst-citation strategy to Icertis |
| concordnow.com | SMB/mid-market CLM, freemium | Medium-high | Bottom-of-funnel SMB content, free-tool lead magnets (contract templates) |

**Gap:** all of the above are built around *post-signature* lifecycle
management (storage, renewal alerts, workflow/approval routing). Their
content invests in "contract management software," "CLM platform,"
"contract repository" — high-volume, high-competition, and **wrong
intent** for someone asking "is this SOW safe to sign." None of them own
a dedicated pre-signature scope-risk content cluster.

## Category 2: AI contract review / redlining

| Domain | What they own | Est. relative authority | SEO angle they use |
|---|---|---|---|
| spellbook.legal | AI redlining inside Word, law-firm + in-house legal | Medium-high | "AI contract review," "AI redlining," heavy LinkedIn/content-marketing presence more than pure SEO |
| lexion.ai | AI contract data extraction (part of Ironclad now) | Medium | Merged into Ironclad's content strategy |
| kirasystems.com | AI contract analysis, diligence-focused (M&A) | Medium | Due-diligence / M&A angle, not procurement |

**Gap:** these are general-purpose across *all* contract types (NDAs,
leases, M&A diligence) — none specialize in SOW/RFP scope-and-delivery
risk specifically. Their content is "AI contract review" broadly, not
"AI SOW review" or "AI RFP evaluation."

## Category 3: RFP response/management

| Domain | What they own | Est. relative authority | SEO angle they use |
|---|---|---|---|
| loopio.com | RFP response software (for the *bidder* side) | High | "RFP response," "RFP software," extensive glossary + template library |
| responsive.io (RFPIO) | RFP response + content library | High | Similar template/glossary strategy to Loopio |
| ombud.com | RFP/proposal response | Medium | Enterprise-focused, less SEO-content-heavy |

**Gap:** these help the *vendor* write/respond to an RFP. ScopeWise serves
the *buyer* evaluating what they received (or a vendor self-checking their
own SOW before sending it) — opposite side of the table, different intent
entirely (e.g. "how to respond to an RFP" vs. "how to evaluate an RFP" —
Loopio/Responsive own the former, nobody owns the latter well).

## Keyword-gap hypothesis (qualitative, needs tool verification)

Based on the category mapping above, the terms most likely **underserved**
by all three competitor categories, and therefore ScopeWise's best early
ranking opportunities:

- `SOW review checklist` / `statement of work review checklist`
- `how to review a SOW before signing`
- `RFP evaluation checklist` / `how to evaluate an RFP response`
- `scope creep prevention` / `scope creep clauses`
- `AI SOW review` / `AI RFP review` (the exact-category term barely exists yet)
- `pre-signature contract review`

Terms to **avoid competing head-on for** (high competition, dominated by
Category 1, wrong intent match even if ranked):
- `contract management software`
- `CLM platform`
- `AI contract review` (too broad, Spellbook/Evisort own it)

## What to build once real tools are available

1. Run domain-intersection analysis (Ironclad + Loopio + Spellbook vs.
   ScopeWise) to find keywords all three rank for that ScopeWise doesn't
   yet target — likely surfaces glossary/definitional terms.
2. Pull backlink profiles for LinkSquares and Concord (mid-market, more
   comparable scale to a new entrant) to identify realistic link-building
   targets (directories, review sites like G2/Capterra, legal-ops
   publications) rather than chasing Ironclad's enterprise-tier links.
3. Track AI Overview / ChatGPT citation presence for the 6 gap keywords
   above monthly — this is a new-enough space that citation share is
   winnable before the CLM incumbents notice and pivot content strategy.
